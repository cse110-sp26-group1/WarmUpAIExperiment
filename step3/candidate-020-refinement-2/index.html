Slot Machine History, Compressed for Modern Attention Spans

Long before AI products started converting curiosity into monthly invoices, slot machines were already perfecting the art of tiny wins, loud lights, and optimistic math.

1890s

Early mechanical gambling devices appeared in saloons, proving that people would happily trust spinning symbols long before they trusted recommendation algorithms.

1895

Charles Fey's Liberty Bell machine popularized the three-reel layout, a format so effective that even today's digital systems still imitate its drama.

1900s-1960s

Manufacturers iterated on fruit symbols, payouts, and automated mechanics, steadily refining the feedback loop that modern apps now call engagement.

1970s

Video slot machines arrived, replacing some gears with screens and software. The machine got smarter, but not in a way that helped the player.

1990s-2000s

Online casinos carried the format onto the web, where animated reels and persistent accounts made token burning a 24-hour international service.

Now

We have AI-themed slot satire instead: same spinning suspense, same dopamine choreography, just with extra jargon and a stronger belief that tokens will solve everything.
